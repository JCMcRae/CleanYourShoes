Lesson 1

Cleaning Your Shoes

Because they look atrocious.

Not too long ago, you probably copped some of the hottest shoes on the market. Because you are a practical person, you put that money to use and you proceed to flex on your family and friends, donning your new wares everywhere you go. But over time, the elements start to show on your shoes, and the once prized posessions of your closet no longer bring you the attention they once did. Fortunately for you, you can restore your shoes to a degree of their former glory by following these steps to cleaning your shoes.


Depending on the number of shoes you have or how many you decide to clean, the process can take anywhere from 15 minutes to several hours. The trade-off, however, is a lifetime of clean shoes in great shape and condition.

Steps

  1. Fill your bowl about halfway with water. Add anywhere from a quarter cup to half a cup of laundry detergent. Stir and mix vigorously to create the cleaning soluton.
  2. Dip toothbrush in the solution and stir. Shake off excess water from toothbrush.
  3. Unlace your shoes, and set aside the shoe laces. Instructions for cleaning the shoe laces are detailed later.
  4. Scrub sections of your shoe with toothbrush and solution. Make sure to dip your toothbrush 3-5 times a section to ensure a good clean.
    • Be Mindful that Suede and water do not get along. For the inexperienced, one should have with them a shoe eraser to clean parts of suede.
    • For those a little more savvy, dip the tip (and we mean the absolute tip; your brush is still virtually dry) of your brush's bristles in the solution, and scrub across the suede to still give it a clean scrub. The idea here is that the solution can work on the suede without the discoloration or ruin that water will bring.
  5. After scrubbing a section or part of your shoe, wipe off the excess water and solution with the rag or towel. Finish drying off the shoe with the rag.
    • If your towel gets to be really dirty or damp, it's best to get another towel, as the first towel gets to be less effective at cleaning and drying your shoes.
  6. Shoelaces:
    • For cleaning shoelaces, dip the shoe laces in your bowl. Squeeze and rub the laces together in the bowl. There are a number of ways to do this; think of old fashioned laundry cleaning.
    • Take the shoe laces out of the bowl, and ring them out over the bowl. An easy way to do this is to hold one end of the lace, and running your other hand down the lace, ringing it as you move down the string.
    • hang dry your strings and let them air dry. A good amount of time is anywhere from 1-3 hours.
  7. Re-lace your shoes to your fitting, and then you're done!

You Will Need:

  • A bowl; clear plastic ones preferred
  • A tootbrush (that you aren't using to brush your teeth with.)
  • Liquid Laundry Detergent
  • A small towel or rag
  • Shoes you want to clean

Maintenance Tips:

  • To keep your shoes the absolute freshest, it is recommended that you clean your shoes every week. This guarantees that your shoes stay nearly as clean as they day you got them. It is also easier to clean them and remove dirt and marks by cleaning them consistently, as compared to letting weeks or months of dirt and grime build-up.
  • To protect your shoes from dirt and rain, visit your local shoe store and purchase water and/or stain repellant. This helps prevent long lasting stains from liquids and grime that would otherwise be difficult to get off of your shoes. Apply these before you wear your shoes for the first time, and every time after you clean them.
