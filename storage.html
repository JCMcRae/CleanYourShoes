Storage

Preserve Your Shoes.

So you know how to clean your shoes, but now it's time to make sure they stay clean when you aren't wearing them. Just because they aren't on your feet doesn't mean they can't accumulate dirt and dust. Shoes just laying around your place can accumulate dirt and dust, along with stacking them on top of each other or throwing them in a pile unprotected. These things can lead to dirt and scuff marks from the other shoes.


To preserve your the cleanliness of your shoes, it is a good idea to keep them in a box of some sort. Many people keep them in their original shoe box. This is a common practice, and while it is acceptable, the manufacturer's shoe box is cheap, can be worn down and broken very easily. One alternative is to buy a container from any local Wal-Mart, Target, Container Store, etc. Plastic box containers are inexpensive, sturdy, and last a long time. While one size usually does the trick for many shoes, consider getting bigger sizes for high tops.

Storage Tips:

  • While Yellowing is virtually inevitable, you can aid the consistent cleaning with storing silica gels in the shoe boxes. This helps preserve the whiteness of the shoes (especially the soles) for a long time. You should change out the silica gels every few months.
  • If you don't have boxes or containers to put your shoes in, keeping them nice, neat, and lined up in rows in a closet is a default and standard option. This means you have to keep your closet clean, however. If you aren't a slob, this shouldn't be a problem.
  • Again, don't stack the shoes if you don't have boxes. The dirt from the bottom of shoes is usually a lot nastier and more of a hassle to clean off of other shoes, namely the laces.
  • Shoe trees are your best friend. They aren't just for your dad's dress shoes. We all know that one dude with the raggedy, lifeless J's, and we don't want you to be him (or her). Shoe tress help keep the form of your shoes, preventing them from looking deflated after weeks of wear. You don't need fancy wood ones, you can find plastic ones for cheaper that do the trick all the same.
