Shoe Kit Assembly

Build Up Your Essentials.

Before you even start to clean a square inch of your shoes, you need to have a proper set of tools and products to ensure your shoes get the best clean possible. Fortunately, you don't need to spend a lot of money to assemble such a kit.


The most basic and easiest kit that gets the job done consists of a toothbrush, bowl, small rag or towel, and laundry detergent. Some people pour laundry detergent in a smaller bottle or container reserved for cleaning shoes. Others mix it with water to have a ready made solution in said bottle or container. If you choose to have solution made beforehand, it is important to refresh it every few cleaning sessions, otherwise it will eventually lose its cleaning potency.


For people with suede shoes (or shoes with pieces of suede on them), it is good to also include a shoe eraser and a suede brush. Since water and suede don't mix, the shoe eraser will get rid of marks and dirt from the suede, and the brush will restore its nap.


For quick cleaning sessions that don't require the extensive clean, but rather a quick touch-up, some instant shoe cleaner is good to have. For the intents and purposes of this website, it is not meant to be used as an alternative cleaning method. Although it is meant to be used as such, we don't recommend it, as many products don't and can't deliver the same clean as the traditional method.


Lastly, some water and/or dirt repellent (commonly referred to as "Rain 'N Stain") is also very helpful. This acts as a protective coat on your shoes to shield them from the elements and other things that may bring dirt and stain to your shoes. These should be applied to your shoes right after purchasing them, and after every clean. Some products make note that the shoes need to be stored for a few hours after applying a coat, so be sure to read the products instructions for further details.

A Good Kit:

  • A bowl; clear plastic ones preferred
  • A tootbrush (that you aren't using to brush your teeth with.)
  • Liquid Laundry Detergent
  • A small towel or rag
  • Suede Brush
  • Shoe Eraser
  • Instant Shoe Cleaner
  • Water/Dirt/Stain Repellent

Shoe Kit Tips:

  • Many brands offer essentials to help build a kit. Jason Markk offers a ready made solution that need only be mixed with water, quick wipes, repellent, and also a brush specially designed for cleaning shoes.
  • RESTOCK YOUR KIT! If something is low or used up, replace it.
  • Keep your kit all together. Get a bag, box, or container to keep it all in one place. It sounds trivial, but it saves you time and stress.
